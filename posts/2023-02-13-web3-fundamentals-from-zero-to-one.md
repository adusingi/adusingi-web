## Week of March 28, 2023

### What is a Bridge?

A bridge is a connection that allows two different blockchain networks to communicate with each other and exchange assets.

You can think of each blockchain as an island, with its own rules, money, language, and so on. A crypto bridge is the term for the networked route connecting these two islands.

In a previous week (Feb 27, 2023), we talked about gas fees. If you have interacted with the Ethereum Mainnet network (Layer 1), you have probably noticed that gas fees can be very expensive.

**Learn more:**
- [Layer 1](https://ethereum.org/en/layer-2/#what-is-layer-1)
- [Layer 2](https://ethereum.org/en/layer-2/)

Bridges facilitate communication between Layer 1 and Layer 2.

**Resources:**
- [Bridge configuration for wallet](https://chainlist.org/)
- [List of bridges and interoperability](https://ethereum.org/en/bridges/)

## Week of Feb 20, 2023

### What is DeFi?

**DeFi** refers to a new financial system that operates on a decentralized, blockchain-based infrastructure, rather than relying on traditional financial intermediaries like banks and exchanges.

DeFi aims to provide open, transparent, and permissionless financial services to anyone with an internet connection.

## Week of Feb 13, 2023

### What is a Cryptocurrency Wallet?

For this week, everyone should have a crypto wallet (desktop). This step is very important.

**Action for this Week:**

1. Download the [Brave web browser](https://brave.com/) (not mandatory but highly recommended)
2. Go to [MetaMask](https://metamask.io/) to create your wallet (your passport to interact with the blockchain)

### Understanding Wallets

To simplify, let me use the analogy of the internet. In Web2.0, to interact with any application (Instagram, Facebook, Gmail, online bank, etc.), you need an email address. This email address gives you access to the application.

- If you **LOSE** your password, you can no longer access your email or the application.
- If someone **STEALS** your password, that person can access your email or banking account.

The power of a wallet is that it gives you complete control of everything you do on the blockchain.

> **WARNING:** You must write down and store your private key in a safe place. If you lose it, you'll lose everything associated with that address.

**More detailed definition:** [What is a crypto wallet](https://www.coinbase.com/learn/crypto-basics/what-is-a-crypto-wallet)
