For a long time, AI frustrated me more than it excited me.

Not because I didn't believe in it — but because, as an engineer, I **need to understand how things work**.

AI, especially Large Language Models (LLMs), felt like a massive black box. And I was convinced that to truly understand it, I would have to:

- Go back to heavy math
- Relearn linear algebra, probability, optimization
- Invest a huge amount of time I honestly didn't have

That gap — between **wanting to understand everything** and **not having the bandwidth** — was deeply frustrating. As engineers, we're not very comfortable using tools we don't understand.

## The Turning Point

Everything changed when I discovered **Andrej Karpathy's content**.

He doesn't just explain *what* LLMs do — he explains **high-level math and concepts behind them without shortcuts, hype, or unnecessary complexity**.

For the first time, I felt comfortable again:
- Comfortable with my mental model
- Comfortable investing my time in AI
- Comfortable using these tools seriously, without feeling like I was hand-waving my own understanding

That moment changed my relationship with AI — from resistance, to curiosity, and then to real adoption.

Beyond that, he's a former Director of AI at Tesla, a founding member of OpenAI, and one of the clearest educators in the deep learning space.

## A Note for Engineers

You don't need to master all the math again to work seriously with AI. You **do** need:

- A solid mental model
- An honest understanding of what matters and what doesn't
- The right learning resources

Once you have that, AI stops feeling like magic — and starts feeling like a tool.
